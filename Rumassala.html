Discover The Beautiful Galle City

Rumassala

Rumassala Mountain, also known as Rumassala Hill, is a prominent natural landmark located near Galle in Sri Lanka. It is a picturesque mountain that rises to a height of about 200 meters (656 feet) and offers panoramic views of the surrounding area, including the ocean and Galle Fort.Rumassala Mountain is known for its lush greenery, diverse flora, and tranquil atmosphere. According to local legend, the mountain is believed to be a part of the mythical Himalayan mountain range, which was dropped in Sri Lanka by the Hindu monkey god Hanuman while he was searching for medicinal herbs to heal Lord Rama's injured soldiers in the epic Ramayana.

The mountain is also associated with various cultural and historical significance. It is believed to be the site where King Ravana's healing herbs, known as "Sanjeevani," were discovered. Additionally, Rumassala is home to a small Buddhist temple called "Rumassala Kanda Viharaya," which attracts visitors seeking spiritual solace and blessings.

Apart from its cultural and historical significance, Rumassala Mountain offers several nature trails and walking paths for hikers and nature enthusiasts. Exploring the mountain's verdant forests and diverse wildlife can be a rewarding experience. The area is known for its rich biodiversity, and you may encounter various species of birds, monkeys, and other small mammals during your hike.

In recent years, Rumassala Mountain has gained attention for its association with the story of "Rumassala Love," a legendary tale of romance and eternal love. The picturesque beach at the foot of the mountain, known as Unawatuna Beach, is often referred to as the place where the love story unfolded.

To reach Rumassala Mountain, you can take a short drive or tuk-tuk ride from Galle city. There are designated entrance points and paths leading up the mountain, making it accessible for visitors.Whether you're interested in nature, history, or cultural legends, Rumassala Mountain offers a unique and enchanting experience for visitors to Galle.
