Kanneliya Rain Forest

Kanneliya Forest Reserve is a lush tropical rainforest located near Galle in Sri Lanka. It is one of the largest remaining rainforests in the country and is known for its rich biodiversity and natural beauty.Kanneliya Forest Reserve offers a unique opportunity to explore and experience a pristine natural environment. The forest is home to a wide variety of plant and animal species, including endemic and rare species found only in Sri Lanka. It is known for its dense vegetation, towering trees, vibrant birdlife, and diverse flora and fauna.

Visitors to Kanneliya can engage in various activities to appreciate the natural wonders of the forest. There are well-marked trekking trails that allow you to explore the forest on foot. As you hike through the forest, you may come across breathtaking waterfalls, streams, and small pools where you can take a refreshing dip.Birdwatching is also a popular activity in Kanneliya Forest Reserve, with over 80 species of birds recorded in the area. You may spot colorful endemic species such as the Sri Lanka blue magpie, Sri Lanka spurfowl, and Sri Lanka orange-billed babbler.

To visit Kanneliya Forest Reserve, you can arrange a guided tour or hire a local guide who is knowledgeable about the area. The forest reserve is located approximately 35 kilometers (22 miles) northeast of Galle, and the journey takes about an hour by road.It's important to note that Kanneliya Forest Reserve is a protected area, and entry is allowed only with the permission of the Forest Department of Sri Lanka. Visitors are expected to adhere to guidelines and regulations to preserve the natural environment and minimize their impact on the ecosystem.

Kanneliya Forest Reserve offers a unique opportunity to explore the beauty of a tropical rainforest and appreciate the biodiversity of Sri Lanka. It is a must-visit destination for nature enthusiasts and those seeking an immersive experience in a pristine natural environment.
