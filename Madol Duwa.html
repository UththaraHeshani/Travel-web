Madol Duwa

There is a small island situated in Koggala Lake near Galle, Sri Lanka, which is associated with the famous novel "Madol Duwa" by Martin Wickramasinghe. The island is believed to have inspired the setting of the fictional Madol Duwa in the novel.

This real-life island, often referred to as "Madol Duwa Island," has gained popularity among visitors who are fans of the novel or interested in exploring the connection between literature and reality. It is a small, picturesque island covered with lush greenery and surrounded by the serene waters of Koggala Lake.Although the island itself does not have any significant structures or attractions, its association with the beloved novel has made it a point of interest for literary enthusiasts and tourists seeking to experience the ambiance and scenery that inspired the author.

To visit Madol Duwa Island, you can arrange boat rides or tours from Koggala Lake, which is located near the town of Koggala, just east of Galle. These boat trips typically offer a chance to explore the island, learn about its connection to the novel, and enjoy the natural beauty of the surrounding area.

Please note that Madol Duwa Island is not an official tourist site, and its accessibility and availability may vary. It is advisable to check with local tour operators or tourist information centers in Galle or Koggala for the most up-to-date information and arrangements for visiting the island.
